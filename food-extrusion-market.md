<p><ul><li>Cold extrusion</li><li>Hot extrusion</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The food extrusion market consists of two primary types: cold extrusion and hot extrusion. Cold extrusion involves processing ingredients at room temperature, preserving their nutritional properties and flavors, commonly used for products like pasta and snacks. In contrast, hot extrusion employs heat to cook and shape materials, leading to textures ideal for products like breakfast cereals and pet foods. Each method caters to different consumer demands, with varying implications on taste, texture, and nutritional value in the final products.</p></p>
