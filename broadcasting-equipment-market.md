<p><p>The broadcasting equipment market is categorized into two primary types: analog broadcasting and digital broadcasting. Analog broadcasting transmits signals in a continuous wave format, often resulting in lower audio and video quality, and is gradually being phased out. In contrast, digital broadcasting utilizes binary code to deliver superior sound and image quality, enabling more efficient use of bandwidth and supporting additional services. As technology advances, digital broadcasting is gaining a larger share of the market, driven by consumer demand for higher quality content.</p></p>
