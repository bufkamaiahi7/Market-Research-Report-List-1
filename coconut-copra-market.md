<p><p>The coconut copra market comprises various drying methods, primarily smoke drying, sun drying, and other specialized techniques. Smoke drying involves using smoke from burning coconut husks or wood to enhance flavor and preservation. Sun drying relies on natural sunlight to dehydrate the coconut flesh, resulting in a simpler, cost-effective process. Other methods may include kiln or hot air drying, which offer controlled environments for quicker processing. Each technique affects the quality, flavor, and market value of the copra produced.</p></p>
