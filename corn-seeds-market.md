<p><p>The corn seeds market consists of three primary types: conventional seeds, hybrid seeds, and genetically modified organisms (GMO) seeds. Conventional seeds are traditional varieties preserved through natural breeding methods. Hybrid seeds are produced by cross-breeding different parent plants to enhance traits like yield and disease resistance. GMO seeds, on the other hand, are engineered in laboratories to introduce specific desirable traits, such as pest resistance or drought tolerance. Each type serves distinct agricultural needs and contributes to food security.</p></p>
