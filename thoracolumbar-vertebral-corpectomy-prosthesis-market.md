<p><p>The thoracolumbar vertebral corpectomy prosthesis market is divided into expandable and non-expandable types. Expandable prostheses can adjust in size post-implantation, allowing for better fit and stabilization of the spine, which is particularly beneficial in cases of varying vertebral canal dimensions. In contrast, non-expandable prostheses are fixed in size and provide stable support without the ability to adjust after placement. Both types aim to restore spinal integrity following vertebral body resection or trauma, enhancing patient outcomes in spine surgery.</p></p>
